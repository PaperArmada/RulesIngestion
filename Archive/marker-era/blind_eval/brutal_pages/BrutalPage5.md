Divine Innate Spells DC 26; 5th sending ; 3rd life seal (at will); Constant (5th) truespeech Cosmic Attunement (divine) The cosmic dragon is always attuned to an underlying stellar force: either photons or gravitons. They can swap attunement as a free action once at the start of their turn in combat and is otherwise attuned to whichever force they choose at any time outside of combat. The dragon's melee Strikes deal an additional 2d6 fire damage while they're photon-attuned and an additional 1d6 bludgeoning damage plus Knockdown (page 209) while they're graviton-attuned. The damage of their cosmic blast Strike is likewise determined by their current attunement, as are the effects of some of their other abilities. The dragon can choose to exclude any creatures from any effect of the aura or action that uses the aura.

Stellar Breath [two-actions](divine, spirit) The dragon unleashes divine power like that from the core of a star or black hole, dealing 7d10 spirit damage in a 30-foot cone (DC 29 basic Will save). The dragon can't use Stellar Breath for 1d4 rounds.

Stellar Mote [one-action] The dragon conjures a mote of stellar energy that shares their current attunement. They direct the mote to a square within 90 feet. While the mote is deployed, the dragon's attunement aura also emanates from that square, with the same emanation radius. The dragon can Sustain to move the mote up to 90 feet or to recall the mote from any distance. The mote is made of cosmic energy, doesn't occupy a space, and can't be targeted or destroyed by any effect.

Stellar Pulse [two-actions](concentrate, divine) The dragon imposes an effect on creatures within their attunement aura. The effect is based on the dragon's current attunement. The dragon can't use Stellar Pulse again for 1d4 rounds.
